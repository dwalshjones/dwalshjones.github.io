My Learning Plan - 11/09/2022

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I'm not sure what I want my long term goal to be but I know that I want to change the world in some way. Having spent a large amount of my life so far surrounded by computers, I'd like to become a full-stack developer and change the world through technology.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I'm really great at getting something done if I really set my mind to it. My main limitation is that if I get stuck on something, I'll usually just dismiss it and get distracted by something else. I am my own worst enemy, and a large part of that comes down to my fear of failure and of letting others down. I've recently come to the realization that a lot of the self sabotaging I do comes from a place of the fear that I won't be able to fix the problem so I don't even bother starting to try.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Definitely my time management, it's something that I know I really struggle with and need to work on. I'd also like to develop my leadership skills as I'd like to work in leadership roles in the future.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives
What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I have set myself up a office that is specifically for studying, I also have three monitors so I can keep my workload across these screens. I've also recently started on a ADHD medication to help myself focus and stay on track.

Imposter syndrome is something that I've dealt with for a really long time so asking for help has always been difficult for me as I feel like if I do, I'm just proving that I shouldn't be where I am. I'm working on being more honest about this with myself and with others.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

Not asking for help has caused me to withdraw from this course once already, I'm commited to making sure that doesn't happen again.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I think my only expectation is to understand that not every student is going to be the same and that struggling to ask for help isn't the same as not wanting it.

My Learning Plan - 5/10/2022

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

I'd like to create something that makes people's lives better, in the past years the world has just gotten angrier and angrier and I don't know how but I'd like to be a part of the solution. Computers and websites have the greatest outreach so this is the way that I'd like to change the world for the better.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I'm getting better at not comparing myself to others but it's still definitely one of my limitations, I'm trying to learn that I do not have to be the main person in the room to be happy with my progress. My strength is that I really like listening to people so I can see where they're coming from. I'm also learning to ask for more help when I need it but trying through an issue myself first but I'm learning that a load carried together is a lot lighter.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think definitely still my time management, I've developed little skills and workarounds to offset this but I'd still really like to meet and set deadlines. I think also asking for help will still play a minor role but I'm definitely getting better at it.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I've never felt confident or that I had the personal skills to be able to lead a team, fearing that I'd let them down so why even bother. I'd really like to work and learn how to lead a team and help them to success and/or through failure.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I'd really like pretty frequent feedback, I think that's largely where I struggled in bootcamp is that I didn't have an indication on whether or not I was doing well or what I could improve on so I felt like nothing I was doing was right.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I would like to be able to balance by personal and learning life better and I think this largely comes from regularly being in contact with both sides so neither struggle. I've discussed the workload that I'll be doing with my partner in a effort for us both to understand what's expected of me.

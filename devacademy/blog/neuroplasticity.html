Mindset

How does understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefit people?

As mentioned in Carol Dweck's Ted Talk and their study in the journal Child Development, at a fundamental level, simply knowing that intelligence isn't just something you're born with and you're stuck at whatever part of the race you started at is. It gives the motivation that if you continue honing a skill or knowledge, your brain can still change, and the understanding that doing this work is what helps create these stronger synapses.

It's also a benefit to know that this plasticity does reduce as we age, so it allows the self-acceptance that older brains won't have the same outcomes as younger ones, but that doesn't mean it's not beneficial, understanding that repetition, undertaking the same practise, again and again, is still a significant part of having a more remarkable outcome.

How might you engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your own benefit?

I'm definitely not as young as I was when I was in my learning prime; even at 25, there are still times when I have to take a minute and think harder about a task I'm working on or why I'm continuing to try to get a different outcome with a method that hasn't worked three times previously but looking at the principles of neuroplasticity it seems to me that even these, what some might call, redundant actions and still having a benefit on my brain it's still firing the same synapses, so they don't weaken.

As my neuroplasticity starts to wane, the way I'd like to engage with the principles of neuroplasticity is one of self-acceptance. Although a lot tougher than when I was a teenager, each step is still helping my brain make those connections.

When I used to go to the gym, I would always see older people in their 60s and 70s who would just absolutely be ripped, and I was amazed that at their age, they were still able to be that fit and lift more than me, at the time a freshly 20 something. To me, that's what the principles of neuroplasticity come down to; it's continuing to show up and lift a heavy mental load, day after day after day.

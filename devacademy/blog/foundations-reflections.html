Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  1. I am so damn hard on myself, honestly I've never met someone so viciously against themselves.
    I need to work on not comparing myself to others because everyone is going to be at there own speed and if I do feel like I'm ahead then I need to put my ego aside and do something about it.

  2. I really like listening to people and making them feel heard. I constantly try and reflect what the other person is saying but sometimes all I need to do is just listen.

  3. I'm not done learning and it's not too late to. My mind is a changing, growing thing and the best thing to help myself is feed it with positive reinforcements, not negative self talk

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Each of these traits intertwine with each other, we bring our values into our learning and into how we program, they allow us to prioritise what is most important to us but empathy allows us to change and adapt our values those around us, understanding that our values don't effect just overselves but the people that we learn and program with. It is only by being self-aware that we can fully empathise by putting ourselves in their shoes.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How much the way we act and our values effect our work and our relationships. They're all very ingrained concepts that in a world of stress and worry we've brushed to the side.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Confronting the worst parts of myself, the parts that quite frequently are the parts that cause me pain and therefore effect those around me. Feeling less than everyone else isn't something that we're born with, it's something that we're pick up and then continue to tell ourselves.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

So much of programming requires using your brain and being fully present, by having the skills to empathise with others and be self aware allows us to be more adaptive and less prone to alienating others and burning out.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

Honestly, I totally thought this at the beginning of foundations. I was so committed to trying to be the best that I set myself up for failure. The moment I viewed myself as lesser or not good as another classmate, I faltered. I thought what was the point of even continuing if I was just going to end up in someone else's shadow. That is why core learning is important. It allowed me to continue on going once I got to that negative dip by coming to terms with the fact that it is okay not to be the best. I needed to be able to learn that programming is not just for myself but for my team.
